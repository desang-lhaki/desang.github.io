Research Question: How did the implementation of new tourism strategies in Bhutan impact the societies of Paro and Thimphu in terms of environmental, economical and cultural aspects?

  • Bhutan is often seen as a peaceful, culturally rich, and environmentally conscious paradise, but growing tourism has also caused serious challenges for locals, including congestion, job insecurity, and environmental damage. Recognizing that tourism affects the entire nation, Bhutan used the pandemic as an opportunity to rethink its tourism model and introduced higher Sustainable Development Fees (SDF) in 2022 to limit exploitation and promote sustainability. While this policy aims to protect Bhutan‚Äôs culture and environment, it has raised concerns among local tourism workers about their livelihoods. This research examines the impacts of these new strategies in major tourist areas like Paro and Thimphu and considers whether Bhutan‚Äôs approach could be applied to other tourism-dependent countries.

Title: Sustainable Development In Castilla-La Mancha, Spain

Research Question: To what extent has the European Green Deal impacted sustainable development in Castilla-La Mancha, Spain for the years 2020-2023 through the production of Wind energy?

  • An economy dependent on endless growth is unsustainable within a finite system, especially as the overuse of non-renewable resources like fossil fuels drives climate change and environmental damage. Since free markets fail to regulate these resources effectively, government intervention is necessary to address market failures and promote sustainable development. The European Green Deal represents a coordinated response to climate change by encouraging investment in renewable energy. This research focuses on how such policies, particularly wind energy initiatives under the European Green Deal, affect economic growth and sustainable development in Castilla-La Mancha, Spain.
